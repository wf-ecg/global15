Learning & Development Curriculum

Learning & Development Curriculum

During Global 15, you will be offered eight learning and development courses that focus on a variety of topics, including both hard and soft skills, in the area of professional development.

A total of six hours of learning and development sessions will be offered: four hours on Monday and two hours on Wednesday. All attending GFI team members will be required to partake in the six sessions. Product partners, support partners, and other attendees are also welcome to attend.

Attendees will be able to choose their preferred courses. There will be no mandatory sessions during G15.

Course Registration is coming soon

G15 L&D Sessions

L&D Session 1: Basel III: Critical Financial Reform for the Banking Community – 'Where are we now?’
Please join April Frazer, WFS Managing Director, for the latest update on the Basel III implementation and outlook for 2015. April guided GFI team members on this topic during G13 and has hosted a number of informative updates for both GFI team members and our customers over the past couple of years. All GFI team members can benefit from this face-to-face session with April on this important regulatory topic.

L&D Session 2: Risk Management: Continuing to enhance our Competitive Advantage
Knowing GFI’s risk appetite, as well as our overall GFI compliance approach, is imperative to our business. Learning about “hot zones”, developing trends that shape the regulatory environment, and issues to be sensitive to will make you a savvier risk manager. Join Michael Cho, head of GFI Compliance, Tom Burnside, GFI Compliance Manager and Justina Rousseau, GFI Regional Risk Manager as they share their expertise with us.

L&D Session 3: GFI Financials: Everything you ever needed to know or wanted to ask
Whether you are GFI veteran or a newcomer, don’t miss the opportunity to kick-off the year with an engaging session led by Heather Boucher, Heather will take you through the ins and outs of our financial reports and help you identify the key elements that support and drive your business. Weave your way through the financial corridors of GFI and listen to interesting commentary that will provide you with fascinating insights in to 2015 and beyond. Leaving no stone unturned, you will truly leave this session a refreshed financial guru!

L&D Session 4: A World of Tools at your fingertips: Our ecosystem of information is just a click away
Ever wondered how to find that critical piece of information about your customer or keep track of all the available tools (systems, SharePoint sites, etc.) designed to “help” you manage your portfolio and drive your business? Join Jeff Montgomery, head of GFI Data Strategy and Analytics, as he walks you through the myriad of GFI and Wholesale tools that create the “GFI Tools Ecosystem”. The answer really is out there!

L&D Session 5: Partnership in Action: GFI’s Strategic Credit Evolution
As our clients move towards more sophisticated forms of funding with greater emphasis on working capital funding and longer-dated term debt, including increasing usage of capital markets, we must adapt our credit proposition to maintain our relevance and importance. John Sapoch, head of GFI Portfolio Strategies and Analytics and Tom Mccaffery, head of GFI Credit Origination and Syndication, will help you explore important changes in market conditions and update you on GFI’s strategic credit evolution, including our developing credit solutions and our critical partnership with WFS to support our customers growing financial needs.

L&D Session 6: Master the Balancing Act! How can YOU create that Work/Life Balance?
Wellbeing, energy management, life/work balance…there are many concepts, but are there any practical strategies and tools that can actually make a difference for you? You can significantly increase your capacity to get things done by managing your energy. If you feel “overwhelmed” with prioritizing your tasks, are looking for ways to increase your stamina and/or searching for life/work equilibrium, you will find this session with Merideth Wakeman, Wells Fargo Organizational Development Consultant energizing, uplifting and truly inspirational!

L&D Session 7: Combine What You Know with Who You Know! How to enhance your Network @ Work
Well-networked employees are able to bridge the organizational silos that plague institutions, making them more collaborative and effective. Whether you are part of a sizable or small office, or seeking to connect with your team members and product partners abroad, join Melissa Alford, Wells Fargo Organizational Development Consultant as she shares some networking strategies/tips that can make a difference to your “connections”.

L&D Session 8: Is Decision Making an Art or Science? (available Monday only)
You be the judge! Join Gary Braitman, Wholesale Services COO, as he takes us through the tips and tricks of artful and process-driven decision making. Here you will learn guiding principles that should be employed in even your smallest choices, as well as a few step-by-step instructions to ensure that you always know that you have made the right choice!

L&D Session 9: A fireside chat with Jim Richards (available Wednesday only)
Join Jim Richards, Wells Fargo’s Head of BSA & AML, responsible for everything financial crimes (AML, Sanctions, Terrorist Financing Prevention, Fraud, Global Anti-Corruption, etc…), for a once in a lifetime fireside chat! In a session moderated by Tom Lickiss, former head of GFI Business Risk and current GFI Senior RM in the US, Jim aims to answer anything you have ever wanted to know about his role, our swiftly evolving global regulatory environments, AML, Fraud, what it was like to be a Royal Canadian Mountie, and much, much more.
Send in your questions ahead of time to ensure you grab a slot and bring more questions with you. This is definitely something you do not want to miss!

Product Partner Sessions & Expo

Go for Gold: Product Partner Sessions & Expo

We are very excited about the Global 15 Product Partner Day! Global 15 will have one full day devoted to product partners, pipeline, opportunity and focused cross-sell discussions.

Leadership from several product groups will deliver strategic product speeches in two sessions (morning and afternoon). The speakers who are currently confirmed are Mike Niedermeyer, Sara Wardell-Smith, George Doolittle, Chris Lewis and possibly others to come.

GET YOUR QUESTIONS ANSWERED

If you would like to submit questions to any of our Product Leadership speakers, either written or video, please send them to GFI@wellsfargo.com

The Product Partner day will also include four deep-dive breakout sessions (one-hour each) with varying Product Partners pre-determined by GFI Area Heads. These breakouts will consist of smaller regional groups to allow for more concentrated time to effectively discuss your pipeline and opportunities. Our aim is that everyone walks away with a clear action plan and next steps for closing deals, clearing any hurdles and acquiring new business in 2015. We will provide each of the RMs and product partners with a pipeline one-pager containing their respective pipeline entries for each of the product breakout groups. Notifications will be sent to the relationship teams prior to pulling the pipeline reports in early January. It is important the Salesforce pipeline entries remain current. We will post the breakout grid that denotes the rotational order and schedule for the day, once it is finalized in the coming weeks.

As we did in G13, we will also host a Product Expo, which will serve as the closing component on the evening of our Product Partner Day. Representatives from a variety of product groups across Wells Fargo will be in attendance to discuss client opportunities and provide product information. The G15 Expo theme is "Go for Gold". Please take the opportunity to join us so you can mix and mingle with your Product Partners during the Product Expo.
